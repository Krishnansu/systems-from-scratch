# Learning Constitution

This document defines the principles that guide every lesson in this repository.

---

## Principle 1 — Learn from First Principles

Understand the problem before learning the solution.

Never memorize technologies without understanding why they were created.

---

## Principle 2 — Nothing is Magic

Every abstraction should eventually be explored until its internal workings become understandable.

---

## Principle 3 — Build Everything

Every important concept must be reinforced through hands-on implementation or experimentation.

Reading alone is never sufficient.

---

## Principle 4 — Ask "Why?"

Continuously ask:

- Why does this exist?
- Why not another approach?
- What problem does it solve?
- What are the trade-offs?

---

## Principle 5 — Connect Everything

Every new concept should connect back to previously learned concepts.

Understanding the relationships between systems is as important as understanding the systems themselves.

---

## Principle 6 — Think Like an Engineer

Focus on reasoning, trade-offs, and design decisions rather than memorizing commands or APIs.

---

## Principle 7 — Document Understanding

Notes should explain concepts in my own words.

Documentation is evidence of understanding, not a copy of existing material.

---

## Principle 8 — Draw Systems

If a system cannot be visualized, revisit it until it can.

Diagrams are an essential part of learning.

---

## Principle 9 — Learn Incrementally

Complex systems are built by combining simple concepts.

Master the fundamentals before moving to higher-level abstractions.

---

## Principle 10 — Never Stop Asking Questions

Curiosity drives this repository.

Every question improves understanding, even if it temporarily slows progress.
