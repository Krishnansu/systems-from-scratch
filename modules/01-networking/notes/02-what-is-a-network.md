# Lesson 02 - What is a Network?

## Objectives
- Understand what a computer network is.
- Learn the fundamental components of a network.
- Differentiate a network from the Internet.

## Theory
A network is a collection of devices that communicate with one another to exchange data and share resources. Every network consists of nodes (devices), a communication medium (such as Ethernet, Wi-Fi, or optical fiber), and protocols that define the rules of communication. The Internet itself is a network of interconnected networks.

## Key Takeaways
- A network enables communication between devices.
- Every network has nodes, communication media, and protocols.
- Networks can exist without Internet connectivity.
- The Internet is the largest example of a network.

## Reflection
1. Can two computers communicate without the Internet? How?
2. Why are protocols necessary?
3. Why is Wi-Fi not the same thing as the Internet?
4. What components are required for a network to function?
