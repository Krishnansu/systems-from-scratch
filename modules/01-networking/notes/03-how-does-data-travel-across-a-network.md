# Lesson 03 - How Does Data Travel Across a Network?

## Objectives
- Understand how application data travels across a network.
- Learn how binary, packets, and physical signals are involved.
- Understand the role of the Network Interface Card (NIC).

## Theory
Applications generate data that is converted into binary by the operating system. Large data is divided into packets before being transmitted. The Network Interface Card converts digital information into electrical, optical, or radio signals that travel through the communication medium. Intermediate networking devices forward the packets until they reach the destination, where they are reassembled into the original data.

## Key Takeaways
- Computers communicate using bits.
- Data is split into packets before transmission.
- The NIC bridges software and the physical network.
- Routers forward packets toward their destination.
- The receiver reconstructs the original data.

## Reflection
1. Why is binary used instead of plain text?
2. Why are packets used instead of sending one large block of data?
3. What role does the NIC play?
4. Why can packets take different paths through the Internet?
